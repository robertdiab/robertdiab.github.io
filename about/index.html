Binary Code follows developments in digital and network technology, and how law and policy help to shape them.

I’m a professor in the Faculty of Law at Thompson Rivers University in Kamloops, British Columbia, with a background in human rights and civil liberties.

My curiosity about tech ranges widely. Some of it is captured here in shorter essays and scholarship on topics that include internet governance, compelled decryption, and digital privacy.

I’m also following discussions about:

  • the role of the net in fuelling populism and democratic decay

  • the fate of platform and surveillance capitalism

  • telecom policy and net neutrality

  • quantum computing, AI and machine learning
